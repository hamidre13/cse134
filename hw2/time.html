The <time> tag

The <time> element represents either the time on a 24 hour clock or a date on a calendar. It is used so machines can easily decipher times and dates from webpages, making scheduling easier, and the overall page more accessible.

Example 1

Your appointment is scheduled for: 10:30. Notice there is no default visual difference between a regular paragraph tag and a time tag.

Example 2 - datetime attribute

Your appointment is on: July 31st. The datetime attribute doesn't have any visual effect, it just used to access the time and date by getting the attribute value.
